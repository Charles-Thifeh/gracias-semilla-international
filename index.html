Gracias Semilla International Nursery and Primary School

A Day Nursery Celebrating Curious Minds

We teach young minds that being curious is a wonderful thing. They have so many exciting life adventures to look forward to – and we want to prepare them as best we can. We encourage each child in our care to explore the world in the way they know best, with their imaginations!

It is our aim that each and every child leaves us improved. Whether that be in terms of speech, social awareness, problem solving – or any other area where your child is struggling. We are here to actively encourage learning through good fun… no matter the stage of learning they are at.

Of course, here at Bright Start Day Nursery, we want each of our children to feel included. That's why we support equal opportunities for all!

Our Story

About Gracias Semilla International

Founded with a clear purpose — to create a safe, nurturing, and inspiring space where every child can flourish from their very first days.

Our Mission

Nurturing Curious, Confident Children

We set out on a mission to create a day nursery in which children between 2 months old and 5 years of age would be able to learn and grow in confidence. We also offer wraparound care for children up to the age of 8, continuing to nurture children during the first few years of their school journey.

Studies have shown that children do better when encouraged to learn through play — and at Gracias Semilla, we've built our entire ethos around this principle. Every room, every resource, and every member of staff is chosen with one goal in mind: helping each child reach their full potential.

Core Values

What We Stand For

Our four pillars guide everything we do — from the way we design our classrooms to the way we welcome each family.

Play-Based Learning

Through play, children develop problem-solving skills, imagination, creativity, language, observation, memory, and concentration. This is at the heart of our teaching philosophy.

Safety First

We have very high security standards. All staff are fully vetted and DBS-checked. Safety and security begins at the front door and continues throughout every area of our nursery.

Inclusive Community

We believe every child deserves equal opportunities. We celebrate diversity, support different learning styles, and ensure every child feels valued, seen, and included every single day.

Passionate Staff

Our team is handpicked for their passion, qualifications, and warmth. We employ over-qualified staff to ensure the very best care and development for every child in our setting.

Ages 2 – 5 Years

Pre-School Programme

A rich, play-based learning experience grounded in the Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) framework — sparking curiosity from day one.

The Curriculum

How We Teach & What We Explore

Our curriculum is structured around the seven areas of learning outlined in the EYFS, brought to life through imaginative play, outdoor exploration, and creative expression.

Communication & Language

We build vocabulary through storytelling, songs, group discussions, and one-to-one interactions, setting the foundations for literacy.

Physical Development

Daily outdoor play, structured movement activities, and fine motor exercises develop coordination, strength, and healthy habits.

Personal, Social & Emotional

Children learn to manage feelings, build friendships, and develop self-confidence through circle time and cooperative play.

Mathematics

Numbers, patterns, shapes, and problem-solving are introduced through games, puzzles, and hands-on activities children love.

Literacy

Phonics, pre-reading, mark-making, and a love of books are nurtured through our dedicated library and daily story sessions.

Understanding the World

Science, nature, culture, and technology are explored through experiments, visits, and hands-on discovery activities.

Your Child's Daily Journey

Frequently Asked Questions

Pre-School FAQs

Everything parents want to know before joining our pre-school programme.

Children can join our pre-school from as young as 2 years old. We also accept babies from 2 months old in our nursery setting. We'll assess each child's readiness individually during a settling-in visit.
The Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) is the statutory framework in Nigeria and the UK that sets the standards for learning, development, and care for children from birth to age 5. It covers seven areas of learning and ensures every child gets a strong educational foundation.
Each child has a personal Learning Journal maintained by their key worker. We conduct regular observations, milestone checks, and share progress reports with parents. Parents are always welcome to request a meeting to discuss their child's development.
Absolutely. We are committed to inclusion and equal opportunities. Our SENCO (Special Educational Needs Coordinator) works with families to create individual support plans so that every child can thrive in our setting.
We maintain ratios that meet or exceed national guidelines: 1:3 for children under 2, 1:4 for ages 2–3, and 1:8 for children aged 3–5. Every child is assigned a key worker who is their primary point of contact.

Ages 2 Months – 3 Years

Our Nursery — Gentle Beginnings

A warm, stimulating, and safe home-from-home for your youngest children. Every moment in our nursery is designed to nurture growth, curiosity, and joy.

Who We Welcome

From Your Baby's First Days

Our nursery welcomes children from just 2 months old. We understand how significant it is to entrust your baby to someone else's care — which is why everything about our nursery is built around comfort, safety, and love.

Every child is assigned a dedicated key worker who gets to know their personality, preferences, and developmental milestones. We follow each child's individual rhythms, never imposing a rigid timetable on our youngest learners.

Baby Room

2 – 12 months. Calm, sensory-rich, ratio 1:3.

Wobbler Room

12 – 24 months. Active exploration, ratio 1:3.

Toddler Room

2 – 3 years. Creative play, ratio 1:4.

🌿

Outdoor Garden

Learning & Development

How We Support Your Child's Growth

Our nursery curriculum follows the Early Years Foundation Stage (EYFS) framework, adapted for the youngest learners through play, song, sensory activities, and loving interactions.

Sensory Play

Sand, water, paint, texture boards, and light tables stimulate babies' and toddlers' senses, building neural pathways from the very beginning.

Language & Communication

Songs, rhymes, stories, and responsive conversations are woven into every part of the day to build early communication and listening skills.

Physical Development

Tummy time, soft play, climbing frames, and outdoor activities support gross and fine motor development at every stage.

Personal & Social Skills

Sharing, turn-taking, and forming bonds with peers and key workers builds emotional resilience and social confidence from day one.

Music & Movement

Daily music sessions with instruments, movement, and singing support rhythm, coordination, listening, and pure joyful expression.

World Awareness

Books, pictures, and play that celebrate different cultures, languages, and traditions — reflecting the diverse community we serve in Lagos.

Settling In

A Gentle Start for Every Child

We know starting nursery is a big moment — for babies and parents alike. Our settling-in process is gradual and completely led by your child's comfort.

Home Visits

Before your child starts, their key worker may arrange a home visit so your child meets a familiar face in a familiar place first.

Gradual Sessions

We start with short, parent-present sessions, gradually building to longer stays as your child's confidence and comfort grows.

Daily Updates

Photos, observations, and daily activity summaries sent to parents throughout the day so you always feel connected to your child's world.

Nursery FAQs

Common Nursery Questions

Babies should bring a labelled bag with spare clothes, nappies, formula (if not breastfed), any comfort items (dummy, blanket), and sunscreen in warmer months. We provide all meals and snacks for children who have started solid foods.
We follow each baby's individual sleep routine as closely as possible. Our baby room has dedicated sleep spaces with individual cots and sleep monitors. We always place babies on their backs in line with safe sleep guidelines.
A key worker is a named member of staff who is your child's primary carer in the nursery. They will know your child's routines, preferences, and development milestones better than anyone, and will be your main point of contact for all updates and conversations.
We work in close partnership with parents on potty training when the time comes. We follow the child's lead and ensure a consistent approach between home and nursery. Our toddler room staff are experienced in supporting this milestone with patience and encouragement.

Our Primary School

Education with Purpose & Heart

Our primary school provides a world-class education for children aged 5 to 11. We combine a strong academic curriculum with an equally strong focus on character, values, creativity, and community — because we believe education is about the whole child, not just test scores.

Our small class sizes ensure every child receives personalised attention. Our passionate, qualified teachers inspire a genuine love of learning that lasts a lifetime.

The Curriculum

What Your Child Will Learn

Our curriculum is broad, balanced, and ambitious — designed to challenge every child while celebrating the unique strengths each one brings to the classroom.

Mathematics

From number bonds to fractions and algebra — our maths curriculum builds deep understanding, mental agility, and confident problem-solving.

English & Literacy

Reading, writing, grammar, public speaking, and debate — we build articulate, confident communicators who love language in all its forms.

Science

Hands-on experiments, observations, and inquiry-based learning that builds scientific thinking and curiosity about the natural world.

Technology & Computing

Coding, digital literacy, responsible internet use, and creative technology projects that prepare children for a digital future.

Arts & Culture

Visual art, drama, music, and dance nurture creativity, self-expression, and appreciation of Nigeria's rich cultural heritage and beyond.

Social Studies & Values

Civic responsibility, history, geography, and ethics — growing children who are informed, empathetic, and proud of their identity.

Sports

Art Club

Book Club

Assessment & Progress

Tracking Your Child's Journey

Primary School FAQs

Yes — homework is set weekly in line with the child's year group. For younger children (Years 1–2) this is primarily reading and phonics. From Year 3 upwards, homework includes maths practice, writing tasks, and research projects. We keep it manageable and purposeful.
